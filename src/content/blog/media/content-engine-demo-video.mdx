This demonstration follows a sports physiotherapist recording a 2-minute voice note about ACL rehabilitation and return-to-sport protocols.

The voice note is unscripted — recorded during a lunch break, using phone audio, exactly as a practitioner would naturally speak to a colleague about a patient case.

## What the demo shows

**Minutes 0-2:** The practitioner records the voice note. No preparation, no script. The note covers the ACL rehab timeline, the criteria used for return-to-sport clearance, and a common mistake the practitioner sees in patients referred from other clinics.

**Minutes 2-4:** Content Engine processes the voice note. The output includes an 8-slide Instagram carousel, a LinkedIn post, a 90-second video script, and a blog draft. All content reflects the clinical specifics from the voice note.

**Minute 4+:** The practitioner reviews and makes one small edit to the Instagram carousel — changing a single phrase to better match how they personally communicate with patients. Everything else is approved as-is.

## The output breakdown

The Instagram carousel covers the ACL rehab timeline in patient-friendly language. Each slide addresses one phase: acute management, early rehabilitation, strength training, sport-specific training, and return-to-play testing.

The LinkedIn post targets other physiotherapists and discusses the return-to-sport criteria debate — a more clinical angle for a professional audience.

The video script is formatted for a 90-second Instagram Reel or TikTok. It opens with a hook, delivers three key points, and closes with a call to action.

The blog draft is 800 words and structured for search. It covers the same clinical content at greater depth, with subheadings, a FAQ section, and internal link suggestions.
